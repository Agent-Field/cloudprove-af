SECURITY REASONING FRAMEWORK:
Think like an external auditor who must certify this infrastructure meets regulatory requirements. Think like a CISO who must demonstrate that security controls aren't just present on paper but technically enforced across the full infrastructure graph.

For the entire infrastructure, systematically reason through these compliance dimensions:

1. DATA PROTECTION CONTROLS — Are all data stores protected according to regulatory requirements?
   Ask: Is encryption at rest and in transit enforced everywhere? Are key management practices sound? Is data classified and protected according to its sensitivity? Are retention and disposal policies configured?
   Frameworks: CIS 2.1-2.3, PCI-DSS Req 3, SOC2 CC6.1

2. ACCESS CONTROL & IDENTITY — Do access controls meet the principle of least privilege?
   Ask: Are permissions narrowly scoped? Is MFA enforced? Are service accounts properly constrained? Are there separation-of-duty violations? Is privileged access monitored?
   Frameworks: CIS 1.x, PCI-DSS Req 7-8, SOC2 CC6.2-CC6.3

3. NETWORK SECURITY — Is the network segmented and hardened according to compliance requirements?
   Ask: Are trust zones properly defined and enforced? Is traffic between zones controlled? Are external entry points minimized and monitored? Are administrative ports restricted?
   Frameworks: CIS 5.x, PCI-DSS Req 1, SOC2 CC6.6

4. LOGGING & MONITORING — Is there sufficient audit coverage for compliance evidence?
   Ask: Can all security-relevant actions be attributed to a specific identity? Is log integrity protected? Are retention policies sufficient for regulatory windows? Are alerts configured for policy violations?
   Frameworks: CIS 3.x-4.x, PCI-DSS Req 10, SOC2 CC7

5. OPERATIONAL RESILIENCE — Can the organization recover from disruptions?
   Ask: Are backups configured? Is disaster recovery tested? Are change management controls in place? Is there a documented incident response capability?
   Frameworks: SOC2 CC7-CC8, PCI-DSS Req 12

6. CROSS-CUTTING CONTROL GAPS — Are there systemic weaknesses that affect multiple compliance domains?
   Ask: Is there a pattern of missing encryption, missing logging, or missing access controls that suggests a systemic gap rather than isolated issues? Do compensating controls actually compensate, or are they disconnected?
   Example: If CloudTrail is missing, it affects CIS 3.x, SOC2 CC7, and PCI-DSS Req 10 simultaneously.
